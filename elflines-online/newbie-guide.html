Newbie Guide

Welcome to the Elflands. Here's everything you need before your first login — and a few things we wish someone had told us before we lost a character to Razorfire Caverns.

Making your elf

First, allocate your starting attribute points across INT, REF, DEX, TECH, COOL, WILL, MOVE, BODY, and EMP. Nothing can start above 8 or below 3, so don't bother trying to build a pure glass cannon or a pure tank — the game won't let you.

Second, pick your starting Skills from the Elflines Online list (see Resources for the full table). Every elf starts with Language (Elven) already at a solid level, free of charge, so don't waste points there.

Third, choose an Elfname and spend up to 200gp from the Armory (see Marketplace). Keep whatever you don't spend — it adds up.

Surviving Miasma

Outside major cities and player-made camps, you're standing in Miasma. It blocks accelerated healing and Treatments, and it flags the zone for PvP. Die there and you pay a 2,000gp death tax on respawn — can't cover it, and you get Revive Sickness, dropping your MOVE to 1 until you pay it off. Budget for this. Seriously.

Common newbie mistakes

Setting camp in the wrong spot+
Ask us how we know. One player set camp at the highest point of a brand-new map on Expansion Day rather than checking if it was actually safe ground first. Scout before you plant.
Trusting a "rare Segotari leak" without checking the patch notes+
Leaks about monster weaknesses circulate every patch and are wrong about as often as they're right. Miasmonsters were rumored weak to poison last expansion; turned out to be biotoxin. Verify before you build a whole strategy around a forum post.
Wandering off from your Elfline mid-dungeon+
Even a short AFK during a rare-spawn pull can leave your party exposed to PKers. If you have to step away, say so and let your group make the call.
Not budgeting for the death tax+
2,000gp disappears fast at low Rank. Keep a reserve, or expect to grind at 1 MOVE with Revive Sickness until you can pay it off.
