PROTECTIVE SERVICES / MSR-2045

Residential security

PRIVATE CLIENT INFORMATION / NIGHT CITY REGION

A private residence is not simply an address. It is a routine, a family, a schedule, and an exposure profile.

Residential Security is designed for clients whose property, household, or personal routines create a level of exposure that should not be left to public availability. The service begins with a confidential review of the residence, its access points, household patterns, staffing, and the degree of visibility the client considers acceptable.

A Militech plan is not a uniform stationed in a driveway. It is a defined operating condition: who arrives, how they are identified, where vehicles wait, what is recorded, which situations elevate to regional command, and what the household should expect when a concern becomes an incident.

REGIONAL NOTICE

Residential coverage is scoped to contracted property and approved household use. It is not a neighborhood patrol service.

REGIONAL STANDARD

Arrival, without friction

Guest and service-staff procedures are designed around known routines, approved lists, vehicle review, and a clear exception path when plans change.

REGIONAL STANDARD

A perimeter with memory

Exterior observation, access events, and relevant anomalies are documented against the property plan rather than treated as isolated moments.

REGIONAL STANDARD

A command that knows the house

Active clients receive a defined escalation chain calibrated to the residence, designated contacts, and authorized response options.

REGIONAL CONTEXT

The service exists
inside its conditions.

PRIVATE REVIEW: A regional advisor documents the property, household movement, recurring exposure, and any existing security infrastructure. SERVICE DESIGN: The proposed plan identifies appropriate staffing, access controls, observation windows, and continuity procedures. ACTIVATION: Coverage begins only after eligibility, terms, geography, and operating authority have been confirmed in writing.
IMPORTANT

Pricing excludes construction, hardening, municipal access surcharges, restricted-corridor fees, and services outside the contracted geography.
